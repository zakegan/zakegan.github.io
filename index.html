Biography

Zak Egan is a young British diver who made international history by becoming the **World’s Youngest Master Scuba Diver**, achieving the highest recreational diving qualification on his 12th birthday. Born and raised in Northamptonshire, UK, Zak began his diving journey at just 10 years old, training continuously to master skills that most adult divers take years to complete.

He trained exclusively with **Northampton Scuba School**, a respected BSAC‑affiliated centre, and specialised from day one in **cold‑water drysuit diving** — the demanding standard for UK waters where temperatures drop to just 4°C. Over two years, he completed more than 50 logged dives, covering navigation, deep diving, night diving, search & recovery, and wreck diving.

His achievement was officially recognised and widely covered by ITV News, BBC News, Channel 4, and local press. Today, Zak is an ambassador for youth diving, encouraging young people to take up the sport, focus on safety, and explore the underwater world responsibly.
